Safety Guidance

Essential steps to protect yourself and loved ones during disasters

Earthquake Safety

Before Earthquake
  • Secure heavy furniture and shelves to walls
  • Store emergency kit (first-aid, water, flashlight, battery radio)
  • Identify safe spots indoors (under sturdy tables, away from windows)
  • Practice "Drop, Cover, and Hold On" drills with family
  • Keep important documents in waterproof, accessible containers
  • Learn how to shut off gas, water, and electricity
During Earthquake
  • Drop to the ground immediately
  • Take cover under sturdy furniture
  • Hold on until shaking stops
  • Stay away from windows and glass
  • If outdoors, move to open area away from buildings
  • If driving, pull over and stay in vehicle
After Earthquake
  • Check for injuries and provide first aid
  • Be prepared for aftershocks
  • Inspect for gas leaks and electrical damage
  • Listen to battery-powered radio for updates
  • Wear sturdy shoes to protect from debris
  • Help neighbors who may need assistance

Flood Safety

Before Flood
  • Know your local flood evacuation routes
  • Store valuables in waterproof containers
  • Keep emergency supplies ready (food, water, flashlight)
  • Elevate electrical appliances in flood-prone areas
  • Install check valves in sewer traps
  • Purchase flood insurance if in risk area
During Flood
  • Move to higher ground immediately
  • Avoid walking/driving through floodwaters
  • Disconnect electrical appliances if safe
  • Follow official evacuation instructions
  • Keep children and pets close
  • Don't touch electrical equipment if wet
After Flood
  • Return home only when authorities say it's safe
  • Avoid contact with floodwater (may be contaminated)
  • Clean and disinfect everything that got wet
  • Discard food that came in contact with floodwater
  • Inspect for structural damage before entering buildings
  • Report damaged utilities to authorities
